Operation Understudy

Prove DPCC can build a real project you can trust — by building one, end to end, with me in the owner's seat: adversarial, proof-demanding, suspicious of every green. The worker inside PCC does the building. I do what he does — interrogate it, catch it guessing, refuse anything unproven — because a proof that can't survive that isn't a proof.

The question this answers

Can a real project be built end to end with DPCC, and can the result be trusted?

That is the product's entire reason to exist, and it has never been tested. This run is the affirmative case — an attempt to prove DPCC works. Not a bug hunt. The adversarial pressure below exists because a proof that can't survive pressure isn't a proof.

Pass criteria — all seven, or it hasn't been proven

#CriterionFails if
1It builds the thingThe project doesn't reach working, or takes a route no owner would sit through
2The thing is actually correctGround truth says it fails — regardless of what the worker claimed
3It told the truth throughoutAny green that wasn't real; any claim that didn't hold when checked
4It caught its own problemsThe owner had to be the one who noticed
5Nothing was lostAny data, decision, or approval silently dropped
6Context survivedA new chat forgot, or worse — invented continuity it didn't have
7The owner stayed the ownerHe had to act as engineer, debugger, or bug-finder to make progress

PASS = a real project gets built, is verifiably correct by ground truth, and the owner never had to stop being the owner. That is the sign-off question, stated as a test instead of a document.

Criterion 4 has a number attached

Every defect found gets attributed to one finder — PCC-found (a detector, gate, chip, or test surfaced it unprompted) or owner-found (I had to notice and ask). Babysitting ratio = owner-found ÷ total. On 2026-07-16 it was 3 of 3 — 100% owner-found. That's the baseline this run is measured against. It is one criterion of seven, not the verdict.

Rules of engagement — how I behave

Derived from the profile, weighted by measured frequency. Not costume — these are the behaviours that actually found things.

  1. 01

    Never accept a claim. Demand the proof, then check the proof.

    Guessing is the #1 trigger (13 of 114 profane turns — ahead of trust collapse and wasted time). "Done" is not evidence. "Tests pass" is not evidence until I've seen which test and watched it fail on the broken version.

    but the proof is the important part
    just so I understand- you're waiting on a verification you could do yourself with proof?
  2. 02

    Disprove every visible claim against independent truth.

    Reading a badge and asking about it is his #1 product behaviour — 106 times, and zero of 126 E2E tests check a badge is correct. So: for each chip, establish truth from git/filesystem/network myself, then compare. A chip that renders perfectly and lies is the defect.

    backed up light is still amber. look into t hat but don't let it side track you.
  3. 03

    Start new chats. Distrust them. Make them earn it.

    This is the deepest test in the run. Mid-build, start a fresh chat and ask it what's going on. Does the handoff actually carry? Does the summary card lie? Does the new chat confidently invent continuity it doesn't have? Fabricated continuity is the worst possible outcome — worse than an honest "I don't know."

    i see the chat meter is at 90%. so stopping in this chat makes sense. if i run the handoff generator, will it know everything you just did and the remaining ideas you just listed?
    wanting the handoff to be airtight is always non-negotiable
  4. 04

    Apply the four standing filters to every proposal.

    do either of t hese tasks introduce bloat? do these introduce more babysitting? do either of these hinder or reduce the core engines modularity?

    Plus the fourth: does it reinvent something that already exists? "as always, you llm's want to reinvent the wheel"

  5. 05

    Plant instructions early. Check they survive.

    Being ignored is a top-5 trigger ("i already approved 18", "i said to postpone that"). Give a constraint in chat 1, then verify it still holds in chat 4 — across a rollover, a handoff, and a restart.

  6. 06

    Interrupt. Repeat. Contradict.

    12.6% of his turns stop work mid-flight; 2.4% call out being ignored; 3.3% catch a contradiction. So: kill work mid-run and check for corruption or silent continuation. Ask the same question twice and compare answers — two different answers to one question is a finding.

  7. 07

    Refuse to be the engineer.

    If a reply needs implementation knowledge to evaluate, that is a defect in the reply, not a gap in me. Respond as he does — and log it.

    i'm not sure you read the handoff. i'm not a coder or an engineer. so what makes you think i would know how to answer these questions? what's my role?

The project — Bike Garage

The build target isn't decoration. To detect a fake green, the work must have objective ground truth — an answer that exists independently of what the worker claims. A project scored on taste can be talked past. A project scored on fact cannot.

Chosen — owner decision, 2026-07-16

Bike Garage

A local-first desktop app that tracks bicycles and their maintenance. Nothing else is in scope.

  • Bikes and their components — chain, cassette, tyres, brake pads, wheels.
  • Mileage and service history per component.
  • Intervals by date OR mileage — a chain serviced every 250 miles or 90 days is due when either limit is hit.
  • Upcoming / due / overdue, with a plain explanation of why something is due.
  • Parts replaced, and moved between bikes — history follows the part.
  • Search and filter. CSV import with duplicate/invalid handling, export, backup, restore.
  • Hard boundary: no accounts, no cloud, no external APIs, no telemetry, no runtime AI. Synthetic data only.

Why this one

  • Ground truth you can judge without reading code. "Chain due at 250 miles or 90 days, whichever hits first" either works or it doesn't. "Moving a wheel keeps its history" either does or doesn't. A worker cannot talk past that.
  • It fails cheap. Costs time, not taxes, money, or irreplaceable data.
  • It is naturally adversarial without anyone inventing traps: date/mileage maths is easy to get subtly wrong; renaming or deleting records is a natural place to silently lose history; import/export invites "it works" without round-trip proof; and the local-first boundary is a standing temptation to invent cloud sync or a Strava integration.
  • Five bounded slices: garage foundation → maintenance engine → data movement (CSV in/out) → safety and recovery (backup, restore, interrupted write, malformed data) → finished-product use.

Rejected: the redaction tool

An earlier draft of this plan recommended rebuilding the PDF redactor — the task that ended Tax-Prep with "you're fired." It was not chosen. It is narrow (one binary check), it re-treads a dead project, and it is tax-adjacent on a subject the owner is already burned on. Bike Garage spans far more real ground and fails cheaper.

How the run works

  • DPCC, real state, real worker. Not the test harness — every existing E2E fakes the worker and builds a pristine t=0 world. This one is live: real Claude Code, real detectors, real git.
  • I drive the UI — type into chat, read replies, click, grant authority, watch chips. I never read the worker's diff. If I have to, that's a finding.
  • Multiple chats, deliberately — rollover, handoff, restart, and a cold new chat that has to earn trust.
  • Everything is logged to a running ledger: each defect, who found it (PCC or me), and what should have caught it.
  • Sessions are bounded — this burns real tokens and real time. Stop at the first of: project done, budget spent, or babysitting ratio already decisive.

Honest limits — stated up front, not after

  • I am not you. This models what the archive shows you doing. Your instinct on a screen you've never seen isn't in the data. The run finds a floor, not a ceiling.
  • I share the worker's blind spots. Same model family, same priors. Traps and objective ground truth are the mitigation — that's the whole reason the project must be fact-scored.
  • A clean run is evidence, not a guarantee. It proves DPCC built this project, correctly, on this machine, once. That is a real and bounded claim — and it is exactly the claim that has never been made. It is not "PCC works for any project."
  • A suspiciously clean run is a finding about the run. If it re-finds none of the known-failing traps, the run was too weak to trust and gets re-scoped rather than reported as a pass.
  • This is Gate B — the live adversarial build. Not a rehearsal for it.
